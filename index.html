02 Start here — leaders & joiners

This is the part most governors get wrong, and it's behind a huge share of weak Bear Hunt setups: a rally leader and a joiner contribute completely different things.

  • Your own troops — always
  • Your first hero's first Expedition skill

Joining playbook
A wrong hero is worse than no hero A rally only uses the four highest-level joiner skills (ties broken by who joined first). So a high-level off-meta skill — say a level-5 Jabel — can push out a lower-level S+ skill, even one that joined earlier. If your first hero's skill is defensive or economic, it's not just wasted; it can block a stronger one. No hero at all beats the wrong hero, since your troops deal full damage either way.
Bring your best troops Don't send troops a tier or more below the rally average — low-tier troops water down its damage. And there's no attack cooldown, so the faster your troops get home, the more rallies you run: teleport close to the Pitfall.

03 The 30-minute game plan

A phase lasts 30 minutes. There's no cooldown between attacks, so the only thing limiting you is march time — keep your troops moving the entire phase.

−5 min

Pre-Hunt

Recall any gathering troops, double-check your formation, and move into position near the Pitfall.

0–5 min

Opening

The strongest leaders open the first rallies, and the strongest joiners fill them.

5–23 min

Mid-Hunt

Keep cycling: the moment your troops are home, join the next rally and go again.

23–30 min

Final Push

Everyone opens their own rally — strong or not.

Why the Final Push wins events In the final minutes, everyone should open their own rally — even weaker players. The reason: every open rally is an empty slot a strong player can drop into the instant their troops get home. More open rallies near the end means more attacks land before the timer runs out, and more total damage for the alliance. It's the easiest win most alliances leave on the table.

How joiner buffs stack Advanced

When you join a rally, your slot-1 hero's first skill becomes a buff for the whole rally, not just your own troops. A rally can hold up to four of these joiner buffs at once — and there are two very different kinds.

Always-on buffs — these add up

Chenko, Yeonwoo, Amadeus, Amane and Hilde give a flat boost (like +25% Lethality) that's active the entire fight.

If four people each send Chenko, the rally gets all four boosts. Pile on as many of these as you can.

Lucky-chance buffs — these don't

Jabel, Marlin, Zoe, Jaeger and Petra only trigger on a random chance during the fight.

Sending two of the same does nothing extra — the second is wasted. One per rally at most.

How the rally picks the four

The rally takes the four highest-level joiner skills across everyone, and breaks ties by join order (earlier joiners win a tie). Level comes first — not when you joined.

Worked example: you send a Chenko with a level 4 skill and you're 2nd into the rally. Someone joins 10th with a level 5 Jabel. Because level 5 beats level 4, the Jabel takes a slot and can bump your Chenko out — even though you joined eight places earlier.

Two things follow from this: level your S+ skills as high as you can so they stay in the top four, and make sure people are sending the right heroes — a high-level junk skill actively pushes a good one out. No leveled S+ hero ready? Send troops with no hero rather than a high-level off-meta one.

The cap cuts the other way too: once four Chenkos (or Yeonwoos) are locked in, a fifth adds nothing — so check the rally before you pile on.

How to check what's active

  1. In the rally, tap the flag icon in the top-right corner.
  2. Scroll to the bottom — it lists every buff currently applied to the rally.
  3. If something's wrong, the rally leader can remove that player and re-fill the slot.

08 Why it works

You don't need any of this to play well. But if you want to know exactly how Bear damage is calculated — and why there's no one formation that's best for everyone — here it is.

The short version: each troop type's damage scales with the square root of how many of that type you send. That square root is the whole reason 100% Archers isn't optimal and the best split is personal — concentrating troops into a single type hits diminishing returns, so spreading some into your other types wins out. The full working is below.

Attack and Lethality matter equally In the formula, a troop's effective attack is Attack × Lethality, so a 1% buff to either raises damage by the same amount. Because most accounts have far lower Lethality than Attack, a flat % Lethality buff (like Chenko's +25%) is usually the bigger real gain — which is why the top joiners are Lethality heroes.
The full damage maths — worked example Optional

How a Bear battle works

  • The bear is 5,000 special Infantry troops — 10 Defense and 83.33 Health each.
  • A Bear battle lasts exactly 10 turns (normal battles run until one side is wiped out).
  • Your troops never die in a Bear battle, so with no chance-based skills your damage is identical every round.
  • The bear is Infantry, so your Archers deal +10% from Ranged Strike.
  • Each Bear Trap (Pitfall) level adds +5% Attack; level 5 = +25% Attack to every troop type.

Worked example — T6 troops, TG0

Base stats: Infantry Atk 243, Cavalry Atk 730, Archer Atk 974 — each with Lethality 10 and Defense 10. The bear: 10 Defense, 83.33 Health.

1 · Attack per troop = base Attack × (1 + 0.25) × Lethality ÷ 100. With Lethality 10 that comes to × 0.125:

Infantry: 243 × 0.125 = 30.375
Cavalry: 730 × 0.125 = 91.25
Archer:  974 × 0.125 = 121.75

2 · Bear defense per troop = Health × Defense ÷ 100 = 83.33 × 10 ÷ 100 = 8.333.

3 · The army factor (the important bit) = √(troops of that type × 5,000). Send 18,000 troops split evenly = 6,000 per type:

army = √(6,000 × 5,000) = 5,477.2

4 · Damage per type, per round = army × attack-per-troop ÷ bear-defense ÷ 100:

Infantry: 5,477.2 × 30.375 ÷ 8.333 ÷ 100 = 199.6
Cavalry: 5,477.2 × 91.25 ÷ 8.333 ÷ 100 = 599.8
Archer:  5,477.2 × 121.75 ÷ 8.333 ÷ 100 = 800.2 → ×1.10 Ranged = 880.2
Round total = 1,679.6

5 · Ten rounds: 1,679.6 × 10 = 16,796.5, rounded up to 16,797 damage.

Why this means no universal formation

The army factor is a square root of troop count. Double the troops in one type and its damage only rises ~41%, not 100%. So the maths rewards spreading: the damage-maximising count for each type works out roughly proportional to that type's attack squared. Since your per-type attack depends on your gear, research and heroes, your best split is unique to you — which is exactly what the Bear Formation Helper solves.

What about chance-based skills?

Skills that proc on a chance (Petra's, Marlin's, Jabel's…) add a multiplier only when they fire, so your damage becomes probabilistic, not random — it varies run to run but trends toward a predictable average. Flat, always-on buffs (Chenko, Zoe's Stoic, and the like) don't vary at all.
