The short version

Joining? Put your best-leveled S+ hero — Chenko, Yeonwoo, Amadeus or Amane — in your first hero slot. That one skill is your whole contribution; gear and your other heroes do nothing. No S+ hero ready? Send troops with no hero — a wrong one is worse than none.

02 Start here — leaders & joiners

Joining playbook
A wrong hero is worse than no hero The game keeps only the four highest-level joiner skills across the whole rally. If your first hero's first skill is defensive, economic, or construction-related, it doesn't just do nothing — it can block a stronger skill from being picked. Sending no hero at all is genuinely better, because your troops deal full damage either way.
Bring your best troops Don't send troops a tier or more below the rally average — low-tier troops water down its damage. And there's no attack cooldown, so the faster your troops get home, the more rallies you run: teleport close to the Pitfall. (Bear Trap uses no stamina.)

03 The 30-minute game plan

A phase lasts 30 minutes. There's no cooldown between attacks, so the only thing limiting you is march time — keep your troops moving the entire phase.

−5 min

Pre-Hunt

Recall any gathering troops, double-check your formation, and move into position near the Pitfall.

0–5 min

Opening

The strongest leaders open the first rallies, and the strongest joiners fill them.

5–23 min

Mid-Hunt

Keep cycling: the moment your troops are home, join the next rally and go again.

23–30 min

Final Push

Everyone opens their own rally — strong or not.

Why the Final Push wins events In the final minutes, everyone should open their own rally — even weaker players. The reason: every open rally is an empty slot a strong player can drop into the instant their troops get home. More open rallies near the end means more attacks land before the timer runs out, and more total damage for the alliance. It's the easiest win most alliances leave on the table.

How joiner buffs stack Advanced

When you join a rally, your slot-1 hero's first skill becomes a buff for the whole rally, not just your own troops. A rally can hold up to four of these joiner buffs at once — and there are two very different kinds.

Always-on buffs — these add up

Chenko, Yeonwoo, Amadeus, Amane and Hilde give a flat boost (like +25% Lethality) that's active the entire fight.

If four people each send Chenko, the rally gets all four boosts. Pile on as many of these as you can.

Lucky-chance buffs — these don't

Jabel, Marlin, Zoe, Jaeger and Petra only trigger on a random chance during the fight.

Sending two of the same does nothing extra — the second is wasted. One per rally at most.

The mistake to avoid

A rally only keeps the four highest-level joiner buffs. If someone joins late with a high-level lucky-chance hero, the game can bump out a good always-on buff that joined earlier — and the whole rally ends up weaker.

The same cap cuts the other way: once four Chenkos (or Yeonwoos) are locked into a rally, a fifth adds nothing, no matter how well-leveled.

So the rule works both ways — if your skill won't make the rally's top four, don't take the slot. When in doubt, send your troops with no hero rather than a high-level off-meta one.

How to check what's active

  1. In the rally, tap the flag icon in the top-right corner.
  2. Scroll to the bottom — it lists every buff currently applied to the rally.
  3. If something's wrong, the rally leader can remove that player and re-fill the slot.

08 Top mistakes

  • Wrong hero in joiner slot 1. Only that first skill counts — an off-meta hero there is a wasted slot. Troops-only beats it.
  • A high-level off-meta hero knocking out an S+ buff. It can bump a Chenko off the rally. Hosts: kick and re-fill.
  • Sending two lucky-chance heroes. Two Jabels do the same as one. One per rally.
  • Never checking the calculator. The formation numbers are a starting point — run yours through the Bear Formation Helper.
  • Skipping the Final Push. Even a weak rally creates a slot for a strong player's returning troops.
  • Camping far from the Pitfall. March time is the only real limit — teleport closer.

09 Why it works

You don't need any of this to play well. Open it when you're curious why the meta is the way it is, or want to judge a new hero yourself.

Damage mechanics — deep dive Optional

Why Lethality beats Attack

Both raise your damage, but most accounts have far less Lethality than Attack — so adding Lethality moves the needle more.

Attack 10,000 · Lethality 2,000
+500 Lethality → about +25% damage
+500 Attack → about +5% damage

Same 500 points, very different result. That's the whole reason Chenko's +25% Lethality is the best joiner skill.

Expedition skills vs. Widget skills

Expedition skills (the normal ones) simply add together. Widget skills can multiply instead, scaling with the stats you already have — so maxing Widgets on Chenko, Amadeus and Helga pays off more the stronger your account already is.

The Pitfall and Archers

The Pitfall's +25% Attack is the same percentage for every troop type, but Archers start with the highest base Attack — so they gain the most actual damage from it. One more reason the formation is Archer-heavy.

Stat priority (mid-to-late game)

  1. Archer Lethality
  2. Archer Attack
  3. Troop Deployment Capacity
  4. Cavalry Lethality

Push these through Governor Gear (goggles and boots first), Research, Islands, the War Academy, Charms and Chief Gear. Long term, T11 Archers and Truegold pay off in every event you'll ever run.
